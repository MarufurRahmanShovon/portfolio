Research Experience

Analysing Different Aspects of CCTV Footage Denoising Using ViDeNN

Undergrad THESIS topic

  • We propose a CCTV video denoising technique that can improve video frame precision by utilizing the information from adjacent frames.
  • Two models, ViDeNN and VBM4D were contrasted on the same dataset in accordance with our strategy.
  • After running both models on the Waterloo Exploration Dataset and evaluating their performance using the PSNR metric, it was observed that the ViDeNN model outperformed the VBM4D model.
  • For this, we used the ViDeNN model to train our own created dataset (Consists of 8000 images from 16 video sequences) and compare its performance with the Waterloo exploration dataset.
  • ViDeNN video denoising performs better on our dataset than on the Waterloo Exploration dataset when tested with datasets containing 60% and 80% random noise.

Brain MRI Segmentation Models Comparison

  • This research aimed to study various segmentation models and draw conclusions based on their performance, specifically in the context of Brain MRI.
  • We implemented Data Augmentation and Data Transformation techniques to enhance the accuracy of our models. So that we can easily avoid overfitting, and also for this, our models will learn faster.
  • We test the models' robustness against noise and artifacts in the input data. Evaluate how well they handle variations in image quality and acquisition.
